Core idea

Most waste isn’t caused by bad forecasts—it’s caused by real-time decision uncertainty during service. KitchenGuide guides prep decisions at the moment waste is created.

Prep now Wait Reduce batch

Positioning

Not an analytics tool. A decision automation layer for consistent execution across locations.

Problem

Waste is a live-ops decision problem

  • Regional fast-casual chains can lose $1.5M+ annually from perishable waste (typical ~40 locations).
  • To avoid stockouts and wait times, kitchens over-prep—even when purchasing forecasts are accurate.
  • POS, inventory, and forecasting show insights, but don’t influence the real-time prep behavior where waste is created.

The gap: decision optimization + behavioral compliance in the kitchen.

Solution

KitchenGuide: Real-Time Kitchen Decision Assistant

KitchenGuide reduces food waste across all locations by giving each kitchen real-time preparation guidance—so every store prepares exactly what it will sell.

The right food, at the right time, in the right amount.

Stakeholder value

  • Regional operator: margin improvement & consistency
  • Store manager: confidence during rush
  • Staff: less guesswork

Product components

  1. Regional Manager Dashboard (Control Layer)
    Compare waste across stores, identify non-adherence, track margin and consistency.
  2. Prediction & Decision Engine (Intelligence Layer)
    Forecast demand per ingredient, optimize orders/reorder points, generate live recommendations.
  3. Kitchen Display Tablet (Execution Layer)
    Simple “Prep / Wait / Reduce” instructions during service.

Manager controls → Kitchen acts → System learns

How it works

1) Predict & plan (before opening)

  • Predicts demand per ingredient
  • Calculates optimal order quantities
  • Adjusts reorder points dynamically
  • Flags overstock risk before spoilage

2) Guide live prep (during service)

  • Compares actual sales vs expected demand continuously
  • Prompts staff to prep now / wait / reduce batch
  • Prevents “just-in-case” cooking

3) Learn & improve (after closing)

  • Shows waste cost by ingredient
  • Identifies over-prep periods
  • Refines next day’s plan

Result: Accurate purchasing + real-time cooking decisions across all stores.

Success metrics

Leading indicators (behavior change)

  • Prep-guidance adherence rate: % followed vs overridden per shift
  • Manual override rate: overrides ÷ total recommendations
  • Emergency prep events: unplanned batches cooked during rush

Lagging indicators (business impact)

  • Perishable waste cost/store: discarded ingredients vs baseline
  • Stockout incidents: count of items unavailable during service

Goal: reduce waste without harming service.

Launch strategy

Teams involved

  • Customer Service: 15-minute onboarding + early support
  • Operations: workflow fit + adoption reinforcement
  • Sales: recruit operators + set pilot expectations
  • Compliance & Security: data-sharing approval + role-based access
  • Issue-resolution: fix incorrect alerts + keep override for trust
  • PR/Ads: publish pilot results and case studies
  • Education: training materials for Prep/Wait/Reduce

Day-1 playbook

Introduce a safe assistive tool—not a mandate.

  • Before opening: setup + team alignment
  • During service: staff signals for smooth operations
  • After closing: review + reflection
  • Success criteria: goals for effective opening day
